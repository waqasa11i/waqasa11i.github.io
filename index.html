I am a researcher at the Division of Robotics, Perception, and Learning, where my work is centered on developing robust localization and mapping capabilities for complex, large-scale environments using consumer-grade LiDARs with minimal human intervention. My research directly supports downstream tasks, enhancing the autonomy and efficiency of robotic systems in real-world scenarios.

With over six years of experience in autonomous navigation, I have actively contributed to multiple groundbreaking projects, advancing the field through novel SLAM algorithms, sensor fusion techniques, and control strategies. My work has spanned diverse robotic applications, from autonomous ground vehicles (AGVs) to UAVs, focusing on precision, robustness, and real-time adaptability.
